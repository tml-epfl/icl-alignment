3. Decline in foreign trade: After World War I, Europe faced economic difficulties, leading to a decline in trade with the United States. This decline in foreign trade led to a decrease in American exports and economic growth.

4. Credit expansion: The 1920s saw a rapid expansion of credit and borrowing, leading to a dangerous level of debt among individuals and businesses.

5. Banking crisis: In the 1920s, banks were not regulated, and many of them took high risks with their investments. When the stock market crashed in 1929, many banks lost their investments and went bankrupt.
